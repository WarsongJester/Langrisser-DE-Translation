<page>
Your units consist of 
commanders and soldiers.

yourself and your

<page>
Soldiers must be hired for

Purchasing troops is a

### 3
Would you like me to explain

### 4
When battle actually begins,
try to keep soldiers in the 
vicinity of their commander.
<page>
The blinking area around the
commander is called the 
Command Range.
<page>
Soldiers within this zone 
receive a bonus to their
<page>
Attack and Defense
statistics. 
<page>
It is very dangerous for a
lone soldier unit to enter
<page>
combat when outside the 
range.
### 5
Would you like me to explain
troop HP recovery?
### 6
A unit adjacent to its
commander will recover 3 HP 
at the start of every turn. 
<page>
The only other way to 
recover unit HP is by using 
magic.
<page>
Also remember, as HP
decreases, so does the
strength of a unit's attack.
### 7
Would you like me to explain
commander HP recovery?
### 8
A commander can regain 3 HP 
if you select the Heal
command.
<page>
You may also recover 2 MP by
doing this. 
### 9
Would you like an 
explanation of troop
affinity? 
### 10
Each unit type has its own
attribute.
<page>
Depending on its attribute, 
it will have an advantage or
a disadvantage in combat. 
<page>
Here are some of the
basics... 
<page>
Infantry are strong against 
pikemen, but weak against 
cavalry.
<page>
Pikemen are strong against
cavalry, but weak against 
infantry. 
<page>
Cavalry are strong against
infantry, but weak against
pikemen.
<page>
Sea units are slow on land
but fast in the water.
<page>
Flying units have excellent 
mobility, but are incredibly
weak to bowmen. 
<page>
Bowmen can attack the enemy 
from a distance and are 
<page>
strong against flying 
soldiers. 
<page>
However, they cannot
withstand a direct assault
<page>
and will likely be wiped
out.
<page>
Consider the map, as well as
the commanders' arrangement,
before hiring your troops.
### 11
Would you like an 
explanation on how to gain
EXP?
### 12
When a troop defeats an 
enemy unit, some EXP is 
awarded to its commander. 
<page>
When an enemy commander is
defeated, all troops under
<page>
its command are destroyed as
well. 
<page>
However, no EXP will be 
gained from such kills. 
<page>
That's why it's best to 
defeat all troops before
defeating the commander.
### 13
Would you like an 
explanation on how to 
position your Commanders? 
### 14
Before conducting your
sortie, all your Commander
<page>
units must be positioned on 
the map.
<page>
Commanders to be positioned 
will have their names shown 
in bright white.
<page>
Also, depending on the stage
there may be times when the 
arrangement is fixed. 
<page>
In this case, you can't 
position commanders, but you
<page>
can still purchase troops 
and items.
### 15
You should have a good
understanding of common 
tactics by now. 
<page>
Don't forget to hire troops.
<page>
A single unit may mean the
difference between victory
or defeat...
