# EN_63 — background fix (clamp stub relocated off the window structure)

**Patch:** `Langrisser1_EN_63_full.xdelta` vs clean JP `orig.bin`
(result md5 `9e3d420aaef3b7a90d1b90550ee7849e`). Supersedes EN_62.

## What broke the background — my fault, now understood exactly

EN_62's kana-clamp stub was placed at `0x0607065C`, in what scanned as a zero
gap near EN_54's ring tiles. Your screenshot exposed why the original EN_46
gap-finder never used that area: the bytes just before it
(`0x60620-0x60656`) are a **display/clip-window parameter block** — the
`013F 00DF` in it is 319×223, full-screen window coordinates. The "zero gap"
was the structure's own zero-initialized trailing fields. Stamping code
there programmed garbage window parameters, and the VDP2 background layer
got clipped away while the text layer survived — identically in SSF and
Kronos, exactly as you saw. Lesson recorded: zero bytes are not free bytes;
gaps must be proven unreferenced, not just empty.

## The fix

Same 28-byte clamp stub, relocated to `0x0607291C` — a gap inside the
class-strings sector that is verified **zero in the original, zero in EN_54,
and referenced by nothing** (no literal anywhere in LANG1 points into it, no
class-pointer-table entry resolves into it). The renderer literal at file
`0x1B320` now points there. LBA 394 — ring tiles and the window structure —
is byte-identical to EN_54 again.

Diffs vs pure EN_54: 27 bytes of stub + the 4-byte literal, nothing else.
EDC/ECC valid on the two touched sectors (256, 399); xdelta round-trips
byte-exact.
